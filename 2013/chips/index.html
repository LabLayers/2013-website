How many potatoes are in a bag of chips?

Let's find out.

One bag of chips

This bag has 220 grams of chips.

One potato

Here is the potato at 220 grams. (It was a strange coincidence that the potato I chose weighed the same as the bag of chips. I wish the numbers were different, so that my equations are a little more clear.)

Slice it

I sliced it using a potato slicer.

Done slicing

Most of the slices are shaped exactly like chips, but a few are half sliced or broken.

1/2 teaspoon of salt

I sprinkled some salt on the potato slices and shook them around in a container with a lid.

Stacked on the chip ring

I had enough slices to fill this 1.5 times. I made sure to include every little misshapen chunk that I sliced. (I bought the potato slicer and chip ring on Deal Extreme for a few bucks.)

Into the microwave

A full chip ring takes about 7 minutes to cook, the half ring takes about 5 minutes. It's a little bit of trial and error.

Cooked chip weigh-in

The cooked weight of the potato was about 45 grams.

Mmmmm

As you can see, I burnt them a little, but they are really, really good! You wouldn't believe that they are cooked in a microwave with no oil or additives besides a dash of salt.

8 potatoes = 1 bag of chips

Doesn't that sound scary?

Conclusion

The potato went from 220 grams to 45 grams after cooking. The bag of chips contains 220 grams, or about 5 times the amount of chips that I cooked from the single potato. I weighed some of my remaining potatoes (5 x 220 grams=1100 grams) and came up with 8 small-medium potatoes needed to create the equivalent of one bag of chips.

As someone who can easily demolish an entire bag of chips during a lunch period, this is a good reminder to STOP DOING THAT. Eight potatoes in one sitting is not a snack.
